### Content

If you've lived in the Phoenix area for any length of time, you've seen it‚Äîthat white, chalky buildup on your faucets, showerheads, and around your drains. That's hard water at work, and Arizona has some of the hardest water in the country.

At Moran Plumbing Services, we see the effects of hard water every single day. From clogged pipes to failing water heaters, mineral buildup is one of the biggest threats to your plumbing system. Here's what you need to know.

#### What Makes Arizona Water So Hard?

Water hardness is measured in parts per million (PPM) of dissolved minerals‚Äîprimarily calcium and magnesium. The national average is around 100 PPM. In the Phoenix metro area? We're looking at 200-350 PPM, sometimes higher.

Cities like Mesa, Tempe, Glendale, and Phoenix all deal with this issue. Even up in Cave Creek and Flagstaff, hard water is a reality homeowners face.

#### How Hard Water Damages Your Plumbing

**1. Pipe Scaling**

Over time, minerals deposit inside your pipes, narrowing the interior diameter. This reduces water pressure and can eventually cause complete blockages. We've pulled pipes from older Phoenix homes that were nearly sealed shut with scale.

**2. Water Heater Damage**

Your water heater takes the worst beating. When hard water heats up, minerals precipitate out and settle at the bottom of the tank. This sediment:
- Reduces heating efficiency (higher energy bills)
- Causes popping and rumbling noises
- Shortens your water heater's lifespan significantly

A water heater that should last 12 years might only make it 6-8 in Arizona without proper maintenance.

**3. Fixture Wear**

Faucets, showerheads, and appliances all suffer. That white buildup isn't just ugly‚Äîit's actively degrading your fixtures and reducing water flow.

#### What You Can Do

**Regular Water Heater Maintenance**

We recommend flushing your water heater annually‚Äîmore often if you have especially hard water. This removes sediment before it causes damage. [Contact us for water heater maintenance](/contact).

**Water Softeners**

A whole-home water softener is the most effective solution. These systems remove minerals before they enter your plumbing, protecting everything from your pipes to your appliances.

**Descale Fixtures Regularly**

For fixtures showing buildup, a vinegar soak can help dissolve mineral deposits. But if the damage is severe, replacement might be more cost-effective.

#### When to Call a Professional

If you're experiencing:
- Reduced water pressure throughout your home
- Discolored or rusty-looking water
- Strange noises from your water heater
- Frequent clogs or slow drains

It's time to call a professional. At Moran Plumbing Services, we serve homeowners throughout Phoenix, Glendale, Mesa, Tempe, Cave Creek, and beyond. We understand Arizona's unique challenges and can help protect your plumbing.

Here's something most Arizona homeowners don't realize: your water heater probably won't last as long as you think.

Manufacturers often claim 12-15 year lifespans. But in Arizona? With our hard water and extreme summer heat, 8-10 years is more realistic‚Äîand that's with proper maintenance. Without it, we've seen water heaters fail in as few as 5-6 years.

At Moran Plumbing Services, water heater replacement is one of our most common calls. Here's how to know when yours is on its way out.

#### The 7 Warning Signs

**1. Age**

If your water heater is over 8 years old in Arizona, start planning for replacement. Check the serial number on the unit‚Äîit usually contains the manufacture date.

**2. Rusty Water**

Rusty or discolored hot water (but clear cold water) usually means your tank is corroding from the inside. Once this starts, failure is coming.

**3. Strange Noises**

Popping, rumbling, or banging sounds indicate sediment buildup. The thicker the sediment layer, the harder your heater works and the sooner it fails.

**4. Leaks**

Any moisture around your water heater is a red flag. Small leaks become big leaks. Big leaks become floods.

**5. Inconsistent Temperature**

If your water goes from hot to cold unpredictably, or never gets truly hot, heating elements or the tank itself may be failing.

**6. Higher Energy Bills**

A struggling water heater uses more energy. If your bills have crept up without explanation, your water heater might be the culprit.

**7. Frequent Repairs**

If you've had multiple repairs in the past couple years, replacement is probably more cost-effective than continuing to patch an aging unit.

#### Tank vs. Tankless in Arizona

When it's time to replace, you have options:

**Traditional Tank Water Heaters**
- Lower upfront cost
- Simpler installation
- 8-12 year lifespan in Arizona

**Tankless Water Heaters**
- Higher upfront cost
- Endless hot water
- 15-20+ year lifespan
- More energy efficient
- Requires annual descaling in Arizona

For larger families or homes with high hot water demand, tankless often makes sense despite the higher initial investment. We can help you decide what's right for your situation.

#### What Causes Slab Leaks?

Arizona homes are particularly susceptible to slab leaks for several reasons:

- **Soil movement**: Our desert soil expands and contracts with moisture changes, stressing underground pipes
- **Hard water corrosion**: Mineral buildup inside pipes weakens them over time
- **Poor installation**: Pipes that weren't properly installed during construction
- **Age**: Copper pipes degrade over decades, especially in our climate

#### The 5 Warning Signs

**1. Unexplained Increase in Water Bills**

If your water usage hasn't changed but your bill has spiked, water is going somewhere. A slab leak can waste thousands of gallons before you notice any other symptoms.

**2. Sound of Running Water**

Hearing water running when no fixtures are in use? That's a major red flag. The sound might be faint‚Äîa hissing or rushing beneath your floors.

**3. Warm Spots on Floors**

If you have a hot water line leak, you might notice warm or hot spots on your tile or concrete floors. Walk barefoot through your home and pay attention.

**4. Cracks in Walls or Flooring**

Water undermining your foundation can cause shifting, leading to cracks in walls, flooring, or the foundation itself. This is serious structural damage.

**5. Mold, Mildew, or Musty Odors**

Water seeping up through or around your slab creates moisture problems. If you smell mold or notice unexplained dampness, investigate immediately.

#### What to Do If You Suspect a Slab Leak

**Don't ignore it.** Slab leaks don't fix themselves‚Äîthey only get worse. Every day you wait means more water damage, higher bills, and potentially more expensive repairs.

**Understand your repair options.** Depending on the location and severity, repairs might involve:
- Spot repair (accessing the leak directly)
- Pipe rerouting (abandoning the damaged section)
- Repiping (replacing the entire line)

We'll explain all options and help you choose what makes sense for your situation.

#### Step 1: Stop the Water (Most Important!)

**For localized leaks (under sink, at toilet, near water heater):**

- Under sinks (look for the oval handles)
- Behind toilets (on the wall near the floor)

**For major leaks or burst pipes:**
Go to your main water shutoff valve. In most Phoenix homes, this is:
- Near the front of the house where the water line enters
- At the water meter near the street
- In the garage (some homes)

Turn it clockwise until fully closed. This stops ALL water to your home.

**Pro tip:** Find your main shutoff NOW, before an emergency. Walk outside and locate it today.

#### Step 2: Assess the Damage

Once the water stops, figure out what you're dealing with:

**Burst pipe** ‚Äî Water spraying or pooling rapidly. Requires professional repair.

**Sewer backup** ‚Äî Sewage coming up through drains or toilets. Health hazard. Stop using ALL water immediately.

**Water heater failure** ‚Äî Pooling around the base, or water spraying from connections.

**Gas smell** ‚Äî If you smell gas, this is a different emergency. Leave immediately and call your gas company from outside.

#### Step 3: Minimize Damage

While waiting for help:

1. **Move valuables** away from water
2. **Mop up standing water** if it's safe to do so
3. **Open cabinets** under sinks to air out
4. **Turn off electricity** to affected areas if water is near outlets (do this from the breaker panel, not by touching wet switches)
5. **Take photos** of damage for insurance

#### Step 4: Call for Help

When you call an emergency plumber, have this information ready:
- Your address
- What's happening
- What you've already done (shutoff valves, etc.)

"Endless hot water" sounds amazing‚Äîespecially when you're the third person trying to shower before work. But is a tankless water heater actually right for your Arizona home?

At Moran Plumbing Services, we install both tank and tankless systems. Here's the honest truth about tankless water heaters in our climate.

#### How Tankless Works

Traditional water heaters store 40-80 gallons of hot water, keeping it heated 24/7. Tankless units heat water on demand‚Äîwhen you turn on the hot tap, cold water flows through a heat exchanger and comes out hot. No storage tank needed.

#### The Advantages

**Endless Hot Water**

This is the big one. A properly sized tankless system will never run out of hot water. Fill the soaking tub while running the dishwasher‚Äîno problem.

**Energy Efficiency**

Traditional tanks waste energy keeping water hot when you're asleep, at work, or on vacation. Tankless only heats water when you need it, saving 24-34% on water heating costs according to Energy.gov.

**Longer Lifespan**

Tank water heaters last 8-12 years in Arizona (often less with our hard water). Tankless units last 15-20+ years with proper maintenance.

**Space Savings**

Tankless units mount on a wall. No 50-gallon tank taking up garage or closet space.

#### The Drawbacks (Honest Talk)

**Higher Upfront Cost**

A tankless system costs $2,500-$4,500 installed, compared to $1,200-$2,000 for a tank. The energy savings take years to offset this difference.

**Arizona's Hard Water**

This is important: tankless systems are MORE affected by hard water than tanks. The heat exchanger can scale up and fail without regular descaling. In Phoenix, Mesa, and Tempe, you'll need annual maintenance‚Äîpossibly more.

**Flow Rate Limitations**

Tankless units have maximum flow rates. Running too many fixtures simultaneously can overwhelm the system. Proper sizing is critical.

**Installation Complexity**

Tankless often requires:
- Upgraded gas lines (for gas units)
- Electrical panel upgrades (for electric units)
- New venting
This adds to installation cost.

#### Is Tankless Right for You?

**Consider tankless if:**
- You have 3+ people regularly showering back-to-back
- You're building new or doing major renovation
- You plan to stay in your home 10+ years
- You have (or will get) a water softener
- Space is limited

**Stick with tank if:**
- Upfront cost is a primary concern
- You have low hot water demand
- Your current setup works fine
- You're selling soon

#### The Arizona Hard Water Factor

We can't stress this enough: without a water softener or regular descaling, a tankless system in the Phoenix area will have problems. Scale buildup in the heat exchanger causes:
- Reduced efficiency
- Error codes and shutdowns
- Premature failure

**Our recommendation:** If you go tankless, budget for annual professional descaling OR install a whole-home water softener. The combination of tankless + softener is the gold standard for Arizona homes.

#### Sizing Matters

Undersized tankless systems are a common complaint. You need enough capacity for your peak demand‚Äîusually the morning shower rush.

We calculate based on:
- Number of bathrooms
- Number of occupants
- Simultaneous use patterns
- Incoming water temperature (warmer in Arizona, which actually helps)

A professional sizing assessment prevents disappointment.

#### Installation Process

Typical tankless installation takes 4-8 hours and includes:
1. Removing old water heater
2. Upgrading gas line or electrical (if needed)
3. Installing venting (gas units)
4. Mounting and connecting the unit
5. Testing and calibration
6. Homeowner walkthrough

We handle all permits and inspections required by your city.
